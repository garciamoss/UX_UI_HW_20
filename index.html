Formed: 1913

Employees: 16,855 (2022)

Department of Labor's Mission

To foster, promote, and develop the welfare of the American workforce and retirees; improve working conditions; advance opportunities for profitable employment; and assure work-related benefits and rights.

The DOL administers and enforces over 180 federal laws and thousands of regulations, which subsume workplace activities of about 13 million employers and over 160 million workers.

Notable agencies

There are more than two dozen offices and agencies within the DOL. The seven most notable agencies are the Employee Benefits Security Administration, the Wage and Hour Division, the Office of Workers' Compensation Programs, the Employment and Training Administration, the Occupational Safety and Health Administration, the Bureau of Labor Statistics, and the Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation.

Discover

Heuristic Evaluations

The initial heuristic review of the home page reveals numerous IA infractions that warranted the DOL’s mediocre ITIF rating. The current page’s design is lackluster, with neither an appreciable color scheme nor meaningful images to engage the visitors’ interest. A tap on any navbar reveals listings of bureaus, agencies, and topics that offer no guidance for the users’ journey through the website; visitors are likely guessing their way. FAQ and Search are present but may lead to intrasite link farms that further burden visitors. An annotated heuristic review of the home page and several top-tier subpages can be viewed here.

Below the Fold comprises Feature Stories, blog topics, news headlines, and Twitter feeds—helpful information elsewhere on the website but not on the homepage. However, the aptly named Quick Links is a practical focal point for visitors to access specific content directly and swiftly.

Usability Tests

I defined three activities that will gauge how quickly participants complete each task and reveal the challenges they may encounter:

  • Find the OSHA page to file a workplace violation complaint form.
  • Locate the COBRA form to receive health insurance during unemployment.
  • Find out which jobs will be in demand in five-ten years for career-planning before graduating high school or college.
Each task averaged more than four minutes for three participants to complete; the fourth partaker quit his search in frustration. Additionally, the average number of steps for participants to complete their tasks is no fewer than six.

Affinity Diagram and User Paths

The group's collaboration on the affinity diagram affirmed users' difficulty finding specific content on the website. Indeed, documented user paths illustrate our participants' labyrinthian navigation.

Persona and User Flow

Usability Issues: Students were placed into several groups charged with redesigning a website from a list of federal government agencies; my group chose the Department of Labor. During the five weeks given to complete the assignment, students learned and applied UI fundamentals and best practices such as usability & accessibility heuristics, UI systems & navigation, and UI & A/B testings. Deliverables included documentation of various UI research methods during the assignment’s discovery phase, a style guide ensuring consistency of various branding components, high-fidelity clickable mobile and desktop prototypes (plus iterations).
