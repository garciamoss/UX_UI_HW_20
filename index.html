Department of Labor's Mission

To foster, promote, and develop the welfare of the American workforce and retirees; improve working conditions; advance opportunities for profitable employment; and assure work-related benefits and rights.

With over two dozen agencies under its aegis, the Department of Labor (DOL) administers and enforces over 180 federal laws and thousands of regulations, which subsume workplace activities of about 13 million employers and over 160 million workers.

DOLabyrinth.gov

The Department of Labor website was created in 1996 as the Internet became more accessible to U.S. households. Though the site has the breadth of services and information for the DOL to continue its mission online, obtaining requested content is often tedious for its users due to an unwieldy and labyrinthian navigation system.

Discover UX through research and empathy

Heuristic Evaluations

The initial heuristic review of the home page reveals numerous IA infractions that warranted the DOL’s mediocre benchmark ranking. The current page’s design is lackluster, with neither a dicernable color scheme nor meaningful images to engage the visitors’ interest. The hero image is stale (unchanged after at least six months) and somewhat off-putting; Featured Stories may be placed here instead, within a carousel, and frequently updated.

Redlined annotations

Usability Tests and User Paths

I defined three activities that will gauge how quickly participants complete each task and reveal the challenges they may encounter:

  • Locate the COBRA form to receive health insurance during unemployment.
  • Find the OSHA page to file a workplace violation complaint form.
  • Find out which jobs will be in demand in five-ten years for career-planning before graduating high school or college.
Each task averaged more than four minutes for three participants to complete; the fourth partaker quit his search in frustration. The average number of steps for participants to complete their tasks is no fewer than six.

Affinity Diagram

The group's collaboration on the affinity diagram affirmed users' difficulty finding specific content on the website. Indeed, documented user paths illustrate our participants' labyrinthian navigation.

Persona and User Flow

Usability Issues: Students were placed into several groups charged with redesigning a website from a list of federal government agencies; my group chose the Department of Labor. During the five weeks given to complete the assignment, students learned and applied UI fundamentals and best practices such as usability & accessibility heuristics, UI systems & navigation, and UI & A/B testings. Deliverables included documentation of various UI research methods during the assignment’s discovery phase, a style guide ensuring consistency of various branding components, high-fidelity clickable mobile and desktop prototypes (plus iterations).
